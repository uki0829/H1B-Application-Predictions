H1B Visa Data Insights

Key Findings

Wage Impact on Approval Rates

Applications with higher prevailing wages show significantly higher approval rates. Positions with salaries above the 75th percentile have an 85% approval rate, compared to 62% for those below the median wage. This suggests that higher-paid positions, which typically require more specialized skills, are prioritized in the visa approval process.

Geographic Concentration

H1B visa applications are heavily concentrated in a few states, with California, New York, Texas, New Jersey, and Illinois accounting for over 60% of all applications. This distribution closely mirrors tech industry hubs and financial centers, highlighting the relationship between industry concentration and demand for specialized foreign talent.

Job Title Trends

Software Engineers, Data Scientists, and IT Analysts represent the most common job titles in H1B applications. These technology-focused roles account for approximately 45% of all applications, reflecting the continued strong demand for tech talent that exceeds domestic supply.

Full-Time vs. Part-Time

Full-time positions receive significantly higher approval rates (79%) compared to part-time positions (58%). This disparity suggests that immigration authorities favor applications that demonstrate a more stable, long-term employment relationship and potentially greater economic contribution.
